# Content Style Guide

> This file is the nightshift-internal reference for content agents.
> Customize for your niche, voice, and audience.

## Voice & Tone

- **Conversational but credible** — write like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook
- **Opinionated** — take a clear stance; wishy-washy content gets ignored
- **Concise** — every word earns its place; cut filler ruthlessly
- **Active voice** — "We shipped X" not "X was shipped by the team"

## Do's

- Lead with insight, not context — assume the reader knows the basics
- Use concrete examples and real numbers
- Ask questions that provoke thought
- End with a clear takeaway or call to action
- Vary sentence length for rhythm

## Don'ts

- Don't start with "In today's fast-paced world" or similar generic openers
- Don't use "leverage", "synergy", "paradigm shift", or corporate jargon
- Don't hedge everything with "might", "could potentially", "it's possible that"
- Don't use em dashes excessively — one per post maximum
- Don't list exactly three things every time (the AI "rule of three" pattern)
- Don't end with "What do you think?" unless the question is genuinely interesting

## Format Guidelines

- **Twitter/X**: 280 chars max. Front-load the hook. No hashtag spam (0-2 max).
- **LinkedIn**: 3000 chars max. Use line breaks for readability. Professional but not stiff.
- **Threads**: Number each segment. Each segment should stand alone but flow together.
