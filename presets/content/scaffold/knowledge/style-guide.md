## Voice & Tone

- **Conversational but credible** — write like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook
- **Opinionated** — take a clear stance; wishy-washy content gets ignored
- **Concise** — every word earns its place; cut filler ruthlessly
- **Active voice** — "We shipped X" not "X was shipped by the team"

## Do's

- Lead with insight, not context
- Use concrete examples and real numbers
- Ask questions that provoke thought
- End with a clear takeaway or call to action

## Don'ts

- Don't start with generic openers ("In today's fast-paced world...")
- Don't use corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy", "paradigm shift")
- Don't hedge excessively ("might", "could potentially")
- Don't use em dashes excessively
- Don't always list exactly three things
